Game Aesthetics Review Heroes of Might and Magic V



Heroes of Might and Magic V (sometimes referred to as Heroes V or HoMM5) is the fifth installment of the Heroes of Might and Magic fantasy turn-based strategy video game series. Like the other games in the series, players control "heroes" -leaders with magical abilities- who recruit forces -often monsters- from strongholds such as castles. The heroes and their armies battle the opponents, who also use heroes to lead their forces. It features a campaign series of levels, stand-alone and multiplayer scenarios.

A hero can learn a new skill, upgrade an existing one, gain a new ability or create combos of multiple abilities upon gaining a new level or visiting special buildings on the adventure map. There are two sets of skills available to heroes: racial skills and regular skills. A hero can have up to five regular skills and always has the proper racial skill. Skills are divided into four levels: Basic, Advanced, Expert, and Ultimate. Each skill is associated with three to five abilities, depending on the race of the hero. Each level of skill gives the hero a chance to learn one ability under the skill. That is, a hero with Basic level of a skill can learn one ability, Advanced level two, and Expert level three. An ability is not immediately learned when the hero increases the corresponding skill level.

The racial skill that a hero learns is dependent on their native faction. All heroes from a faction learn the same racial skill and racial abilities. Each racial skill culminates in an 'ultimate ability' that can only be gained by learning a very specific set of regular skills and abilities. In addition to the three levels of skills that apply to regular skills, the racial skill can reach an ultimate level when a certain artifact is worn by the hero.

It has two different modes as Map-mode and War-mode.

Menu Design

  • Tone / Light:
    • It is reflective control as bright look. And the menu has coincidence of tone also.
  • Color:
    • There is a Split Complementary Hue with the colors red, yellow and blue. It is not very saturated scene. It uses warm colors.
  • Contrast / Affinity:
    • There is a contrast of saturation in the scene. There can be seen the yellow is very saturated besides the other colors in the scene.
  • Space / Screen Division:
    • There is a golden ratio between the menu Buttons and background.
  • Placement:
    • Menu Buttons are at bottom rightmost corner as the ususal mapping for the games.
  • Font:
    • There are two fonts in the menu; one is the logo font and the other is the buttons' font which is italic and like hand-writing type. It is coherent with the container for the buttons which is like a parchment.



Map-Mode Design

  • Tone / Light:
    • It is reflective control as bright look. And the it has coincidence of tone. The characters' expressions and faces can easily be seen.
  • Color:
    • There is a green based color palette. There is no visible color scheme. There a lot of game element in this mode and all of them comes from a limited color palette but not from a particular scheme.
  • Contrast / Affinity:
    • There is contrast of hue in the scene.
  • Space / Screen Division:
    • The screen can be seen from the top-down view. There is a virtual aerial diffusion on this scene because the map does not show the area the character has not yet visited. In-game menu buttons has a golden ratio with the whole scene.
  • Narration:
    • Player can choose who he fights in the map-mode excluding the main story enemies. He must face them in any case.



War-Mode Design

  • Tone / Light:
    • It is reflective control as bright look. And the it has coincidence of tone. The characters' expressions and faces can easily be seen.
  • Color:
    • There is a green based color palette. There is no visible color scheme. There a lot of game element in this mode and all of them comes from a limited color palette but not from a particular scheme. However, there can be seen that cooler colors than the map-mode are used in the the war-mode.
  • Contrast / Affinity:
    • There is contrast of hue in the scene.
  • Space / Screen Division:
    • There is a 1:4 Horizontal Ratio between the menu items and the game screen.
  • Movement:
    • There are grids in the combat area and it tells us that this is the main battle arena.
  • Unity:
    • The grids created it between the fighting creatures.
  • Emphasis:
    • It is on the combat area because of the grids also.



Character / Artifact Design

  • Tone / Light:
    • It changes according to the character or the artifact. There is a reddish tone in the Inferno faction which has creatures from hell, and there is a much more blueish tone in the Haven faction which is the peaceful human race. The Slyvan Faction which consists of elven people has a greenier tone and so on with the other factions. The bad factions have less lighting than the good factions.
  • Color:
    • It is coherent with their cities' and factions' designs. While the Inferno creatures has a red and black-grey color scheme, the Haven faction has a light blue, golden yellow and white color scheme.
  • Harmony:
    • They are in harmony with their cities' and factions' designs.
  • Figure / Ground:
    • They can easily distinguished with the background with their color schemes.



City / Building Design

  • Tone / Light:
    • Incident Bright look is at hand in this city and buildings designs also.
  • Color:
    • It is coherent with their characters' and factions' designs.
  • Contrast / Affinity:
    • There is a contrast of saturation at the city design.
  • Space / Screen Division:
    • There is an aerial (the fog) and a textural diffusion at the city design. Farther you look, lesser you see the details and the colors of the buildings.
  • Unity:
    • The buildings are united with the city. There are mostly some statues over the buildings which reflect these buildings' area of usage.
  • Emphasis:
    • It is on the building selected when we enter into the city.



Cover Design

  • Tone / Light:
    • We can see the cover inspired with the incident control. There is a bright light at the back of the character; the character's and the city's color tones are determined by this lights. Also the character is in coincidence of tone.
  • Color:
    • There are three main colors in the cover: Yellow, Blue and Green. It is an analogic color scheme because this three colors are neinghbors on the color wheel.
  • Space / Screen Division:
    • There is a 1:1 Vertical Ratio between the main character and background of the cover.
  • Placement
    • There is golden ratio between the character's head including torso and the whole cover.
  • Movement:
    • There is a latin cross figure on the character's helmet, so it attracts attention to that area. Also the city's towers are very sharp and point to the name of the game.
  • Font:
    • The name of the game is written with a metalic grey and yellow font. Its number is written in Roman numeral type.
  • Emphasis:
    • It is on the main character on the cover's right hand side.
  • Unity:
    • The character on the cover looks like a holy knight from a fantasy world, and in the background there is a flying beast on the sky and the city is not structured as today's buildings, so there is very good bond and unity between the character and the whole cover's story.
  • Mood:
    • The character is looking to the camera, hence he has a demand mood.
  • Figure / Ground:
    • The character distinguishly seperated from the background with perspective, diffusion and focus differences.
  • Distance:
    • We are in the personal distance of the character on the cover.
  • Framing:
    • It is between medium and medium full shot.
  • Camera Angle:
    • It is a subjective camera angle and the character looks to the camera from straight on angle.
  • Symbolic Meanings:
    • The character is between the center and margin.
